# Feature FFFF: Clear Feature Name

## Status

Draft

Use `Draft`, `Ready for Tasking`, `In Progress`, `Deployment`, `Retrospective`, or `Complete` according to the Feature lifecycle. `Ready for Tasking` is transitional while the approved Task set is created; successful `mat-feature` completion moves the Feature to `In Progress`. Final `mat-next` moves it to `Deployment`; `mat-retro` begins only after merge, deployment, and production validation are confirmed.

Audience: business stakeholders, the product owner, and the engineering team. Keep this document comprehensive and shareable; implementation decomposition belongs in the Task Specifications.

## Executive Summary

A concise, audience-neutral explanation suitable for stakeholder communication, email, or a work-tracking system.

## Problem or Opportunity

What needs to change, why it matters, and the relevant current context.

## Desired Outcome

What should be true for users, stakeholders, or the business when the feature is delivered.

## Users and Stakeholders

Who is affected, who requested the work, and who must review or approve relevant outcomes.

## Current Behavior

How the product or process behaves today.

## Proposed Behavior

The intended feature behavior and user experience without decomposing it into implementation Tasks.

## Scope

Everything explicitly included in the feature.

## Non-Goals

Everything explicitly excluded from the feature.

## Requirements and Business Rules

Functional requirements, business rules, permissions, and other required behavior.

## User Flow

Important user or system flows when applicable.

## Data, Permissions, and Integrations

Relevant data, persistence, access control, external services, and integration behavior.

## Edge Cases and Failure Behavior

Known edge cases, invalid states, recovery behavior, and expected failure handling.

## Constraints and Dependencies

Project conventions, compatibility, security, performance, migration, dependency, or sequencing constraints.

## Success Criteria

Observable feature-level outcomes that demonstrate the desired result.

## Testing and Validation Strategy

High-level validation expectations that will guide Task-level test requirements.

## Delivery and Release Considerations

Hosting, configuration, rollout, deployment, communication, monitoring, rollback, or operational considerations.

## Decisions

Confirmed product, scope, behavior, and constraint decisions from discovery.

## Open Questions

Unresolved questions. Identify which questions prevent `Ready for Tasking`.
